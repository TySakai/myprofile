SolPOD

The low-cost SolPOD's simple design consisted of an exterior skirt and a drying chamber to hold food. Cold air would enter the SolPOD through the bottom of it's skirts and be heated while traveling up them. As the air in the skirts heated up it would create a pressure differential which would pull more cool air in. The hot air would eventually move up into the food storage unit and pass over any food placed inside. The hot air would absorb the up the skirts of the SolPOD eventually passing over the dried food in the top chamber.
