LCD Proportion Integral Derivitive Controller Setup

Our calibration system consisted of heating a small cut-out of liquid crystal (LC) to a, known temperature, taking pictures of the LC sheet, and then recording the color that the sheet turned at the predetermined temperature. To ensure that the copper block that would heat the LC sheet heated up at a constant rate and did not overshoot the target temperature we employed the use of a Proportion Integral Derivative (PID) controller. I was tasked with finding and optimizing these PID gains. Using Simulink I was able to model our system and determine the correct gains and was able to reduce the overshoot to less than 0.5 degrees celcius.
